Essential Tools for Scuba Diving

By Wikipedia April 5, 2025
2 mins read

What is a Scuba Set?

A scuba set, originally just scuba (an acronym for self-contained underwater breathing apparatus), is any breathing apparatus that is entirely carried by an underwater diver and provides the diver with breathing gas at the ambient pressure. Scuba is overwhelmingly the most common underwater breathing system used by recreational divers, and is also used in professional diving when it provides advantages, usually of improved mobility and range, over surface-supplied diving systems, and is allowed by the relevant code of practice.

Components of a Scuba Set

The defining equipment of a scuba set is the diving regulator and the diving cylinder. The regulator typically has a primary first stage which connects to the cylinder valve, and a primary second stage connected to the first stage by a low pressure hose, from which the diver breathes, and usually a secondary second stage (the "octopus") for emergency use by a buddy diver, a submersible pressure gauge to show remaining gas pressure, and a low pressure hose to supply a buoyancy compensator. The cylinder is usually a single aluminium or steel pressure vessel, but may be a set of cylinders bolted together with a manifold, or a set of independent cylinders carried as a pony set or side-mount configuration.

How a Scuba Set Operates

The most common scuba set configuration in use is the "single-hose" open circuit demand system, in which compressed breathing gas, usually air, is stored in a cylinder carried on the diver's back, and the pressure is reduced to ambient by a two-stage regulator. The first stage reduces the high cylinder pressure to an intermediate pressure, and the second stage, which is in the diver's mouthpiece, reduces the intermediate pressure to ambient pressure on demand as the diver inhales. The exhaled gas is exhausted directly to the environment through the regulator exhaust valve, producing a flow of bubbles with each breath.
